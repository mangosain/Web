"Harry Potter" is a popular fantasy series written by J.K. Rowling. The story revolves around a young boy named Harry Potter who discovers that he is a wizard on his 11th birthday. He learns that his parents were powerful wizards who were killed by the dark wizard Lord Voldemort. Harry attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where he forms friendships with Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger. Together, they navigate the challenges of school, learn magic, and unravel the mysteries of the wizarding world. Throughout the series, Harry confronts numerous dangers, including encounters with dark creatures, complex magical tasks, and battles against Voldemort and his followers, known as Death Eaters. The overarching plot follows Harry's quest to defeat Voldemort and save the wizarding world from his tyranny. The books explore themes of friendship, love, bravery, sacrifice, and the struggle between good and evil. As the series progresses, the characters mature, and the stakes become higher. The "Harry Potter" series consists of seven books: "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" (or "Sorcerer's Stone" in the US), "Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets," "Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban," "Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire," "Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix," "Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince," and "Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows." The series has become a cultural phenomenon, captivating millions of readers worldwide and inspiring movies, merchandise, and a dedicated fan community.
